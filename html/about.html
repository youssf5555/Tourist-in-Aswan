Temple of Philae

The Temple of Philae is one of the most beautiful and important tourist attractions in Aswan, and it has great popularity and attractiveness in the world. It was saved from drowning by a UNESCO initiative during the construction of the High Dam in the sixties of the last century, and was transferred from Philae Island to Gilika Island, south of Lake Nasser. The highly complex process of saving the temples took nearly 9 years, and a large number of countries in the world participated in it, and advanced technological methods were used.

High Dam

The High Dam is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Aswan, and this huge building is in addition to the greatness of its design and importance, as it tells the story of the struggle and struggle of the Egyptian people in order to achieve a dream that they have long wished to achieve, and it was the biggest challenge facing the Egyptian revolution in 1952, in addition to being considered the largest engineering building in the world. world in the twentieth century.

Unfinished obelisk

The missing obelisk is located in the southern part of the Egyptian quarries, and it is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Aswan, as the longest Egyptian obelisks ever extend on the ground at the site of its creation, to be a testament to the accuracy and ability of the Egyptian manufacturer in dealing with giant granite stones, and it is likely that this The obelisk was carved during the reign of Queen Hatshepsut, as most obelisks were built in her era, but a crack appeared in the body of the obelisk, which led to the cessation of its completion, so that it would remain a trace on the ground.

Nubian Museum

>This museum presents a special and very important aspect of the ancient Nubian civilization, which was associated with the ancient Egyptian civilization. The Egyptian architect Mahmoud al-Hakim excelled in its implementation, conveying with all honesty and ability the authentic Nubian spirit, and the vocabulary of life in it in a manner that amazed the world using the surrounding environmental rocks, drawing inspiration from prehistoric caves with their primitive inscriptions, and models of the Nubian house with its artistic touches, inscriptions, and bright colors.

Elephantine island

Elephantine Island has a unique geographical location and stunning landscapes carried by the Nile River, with which many tourist attractions meet in Aswan of successive beauty and history from different eras, where the tourist enjoys visiting the temples of Thutmose III from the Old Kingdom, the Temple of Khnum, Avonis III, and the Greco-Roman cemetery, As for the rest of the antiquities on the island, several articles will not suffice us to talk about them.

Nubian Village

The Nubian village was able to be a factor attracting tourism in Aswan through its distinction in presenting a vivid and realistic picture of Nubian life, as the Nubian civilization often differed from the ancient Egyptian civilization in the way of life and the distinctive character in clothing and food, the design and decoration of houses, and the use of materials of the surrounding environment in building their homes. To achieve complete harmony with it, and to provide a healthy, refreshing, self-conditioned atmosphere away from the sweltering heat that is the character of the country, so it deserves to be included among the tourist places in Egypt.

Aswan Museum

The antiquities of the ancient Egyptians are spread in Aswan in a way that is difficult to count, and the establishment of the Aswan Museum located on the island of Elephantine was one of the attempts to combine a number of ancient monuments in one place, as the villa of the engineer William Wilcox was chosen, and he is one of the engineers who supervised the construction of the ancient Aswan Dam, and during Excavation work required for the construction of the High Dam, many antiquities dating back to different eras were discovered, and they were included in the museum's holdings.

Mausoleum of Aga Khan

The Aga Khan shrine is located 2.5 miles from the Aswan train station. It was built by “Muhammad Shah”, known as “Aga Khan III”, and he was the spiritual leader of the Ismaili Shiite sect in India, and Imam No. 48 in 1885, and he was one of the richest men in the world.

Qubbet el-Hawa

Qubbat al-Hawa is the tombs of the nobles in the ancient era, and this name was given to it in relation to the “Dome of Ali Ibn al-Hawa,” which rises 180 meters.

El Nabatat Island

The island of plants is one of the most wonderful things in the island of plants, as it creates the atmosphere for its growth in green houses and plastic greenhouses, and it includes 380 species of plants, as it contains a group of fruits and trees, medicinal and aromatic plants, ornamental plants, wood trees and spices.
